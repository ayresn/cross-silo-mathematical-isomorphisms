## 3. CORE MATHEMATICAL PARALLELISM
In quantum chaos, the Schrödinger equation governs the time evolution of the wave function:
```math
i\hbar \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial t} = \hat{H} \psi
```
where \(\psi\) is the wave function, \(t\) is time, \(\hbar\) is the reduced Planck constant, and \(\hat{H}\) is the Hamiltonian operator.

In turbulent flow, the Navier-Stokes equations describe the motion of the velocity field:
```math
\frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) \mathbf{u} = -\frac{1}{\rho} \nabla p + \nu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u}
```
where \(\mathbf{u}\) is the velocity field, \(t\) is time, \(\rho\) is the fluid density, \(p\) is the pressure, and \(\nu\) is the kinematic viscosity.

These equations map onto each other through a latent space transformation where the wave function \(\psi\) corresponds to the velocity field \(\mathbf{u}\), and the Hamiltonian operator \(\hat{H}\) corresponds to the convective and diffusive terms in the Navier-Stokes equations.
