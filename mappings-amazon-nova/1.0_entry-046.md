## 3. CORE MATHEMATICAL PARALLELISM
In nonlinear optics, modulation instability is modeled using the nonlinear Schrödinger equation:
```math
i\frac{\partial A}{\partial z} + \frac{\beta_2}{2}\frac{\partial^2 A}{\partial T^2} + \gamma |A|^2 A = 0
```
where \(A\) is the complex envelope of the optical field, \(z\) is the propagation distance, \(T\) is the retarded time, \(\beta_2\) is the group velocity dispersion, and \(\gamma\) is the nonlinear coefficient.

In fluid turbulence, vortex stretching is described by the vorticity equation:
```math
\frac{\partial \omega}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla)\omega = (\omega \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{u}
```
where \(\omega\) is the vorticity vector, \(t\) is time, \(\mathbf{u}\) is the velocity field, and \(\nabla\) is the gradient operator.

These equations map onto each other through a latent space transformation where the optical field envelope \(A\) corresponds to the vorticity vector \(\omega\), and the propagation distance \(z\) corresponds to time \(t\).
